01. About Me

I'm a graduate student at Stanford University pursuing an MS in Aeronautics & Astronautics with a perfect 4.0 GPA, supported by the Narotam Sekhsaria, KC Mahindra, and JITO JEAP fellowships. My research at the Stanford Multi-Robot Systems Lab focuses on semantic feature extraction for autonomous navigation using egocentric vision data from 3D Gaussian Splats.

My work sits at the intersection of robotics, autonomous systems, and AI — building intelligent systems that perceive, reason, and act in complex environments. From language-steered autonomous driving to hierarchical reinforcement learning for robotic control, I'm passionate about pushing the boundaries of robot autonomy.

Prior to Stanford, I graduated top of my class from VIT with a BS in Mechanical Engineering, where I led a 25-member robotics team deploying a fleet of 7 UAVs and robots. I've also gained industry experience at Boeing, Tata Motors, and India's Aeronautical Development Agency.

02. Projects

LangDrive: Language-Steered Autonomous Driving

Developed a hierarchical autonomy stack integrating Vision-Language Models (VLMs) for high-level decision making in autonomous vehicles within the CARLA simulator. Implemented a natural language interface using LLaVA VLM and RGB camera feed to process human commands, enabling safety-aware reasoning and temporal maneuvers.

Key Outcome: Successfully validated the "See, Decide, Act" loop in complex urban environments — safe behavior in ~75% of cases with prompting alone.

Nov 2025

Hierarchical RL for Robust Robotic Control

Architected a decoupled policy stack using Soft Actor-Critic (SAC) for high-level planning and PPO for low-level force control, modeled as a POMDP to handle high perception noise. Designed custom dense reward functions with stability heuristics for sim-to-real transfer.

Key Outcome: Achieved 2x average stack height vs. discrete baselines (27.18 vs 14.85 blocks), outperforming non-hierarchical methods by 5x.
PythonPyBulletSACPPOPOMDP
Sep 2025

Autonomous Ground Robot: Full-Stack Navigation

Built a modular on-robot autonomy stack in ROS2/C++, implementing sensor fusion via EKF and robust global localization using Particle Filters (MCL). Integrated YOLO-based perception with Occupancy Grid Mapping and a hybrid A*-DWA planner for safe navigation in cluttered environments.

Key Outcome: Successfully achieved self-navigation in dynamic environments with robust sensor and path-planning integration.
