01. Research

I'm a graduate student at Stanford University pursuing an MS in Aeronautics & Astronautics, supported by three national fellowships. My research sits at the intersection of robotics, perception, and natural language — building intelligent systems that understand their environment semantically and act on complex human instructions. Prior to Stanford, I graduated top of my class from VIT with a BS in Mechanical Engineering, led a 25-member university robotics team deploying 7 UAVs and robots, and held engineering roles at Boeing and Tata Motors.

StarNav: GPS-Free Lunar Rover Localization

  • GPS-free localization via star pattern recognition and probabilistic inference from sky imagery
  • Full pipeline: multi-threshold blob detection → coordinate transforms → lunar ENU frames → geometric star triangle signatures
  • Particle filter localization across a 200 km search region from sky imagery alone
Key Outcome: ~40 km localization accuracy. Star detection improved from 60% to 100%; triangle signatures expanded from 20 to 364, reducing search space from 38M km² to 126k km².
